Students must maintain a minimum attendance of 75% in every subject during the semester. Attendance is reviewed monthly by faculty advisors, and students with low attendance may receive warning notices. Students failing to meet the minimum attendance requirement may not be permitted to appear for semester examinations unless valid medical certificates or official permissions are provided.

The college library contains over 50,000 academic books, journals, magazines, newspapers, digital archives, and research papers across multiple disciplines. Students can borrow up to 3 books for a duration of two weeks. The library also provides reading halls, internet-enabled computers, quiet study zones, and Wi-Fi access for research and project work.

The placement cell organizes aptitude training, technical workshops, resume-building sessions, mock interviews, group discussion practice, and coding bootcamps to prepare students for campus recruitment. Top companies from software, consulting, manufacturing, and finance sectors visit the campus every year for placements and internships.

Scholarship forms are distributed through the administration office at the beginning of each semester. Merit-based scholarships are awarded to academically outstanding students, while financial assistance programs are available for students from economically weaker backgrounds. Required documents include income certificates, previous semester marks cards, and identity proof.

The exam cell is located in Block B near the administrative section of the campus. Students can contact the exam cell regarding hall tickets, exam schedules, internal marks, supplementary examinations, revaluation procedures, and examination fee payments. Notifications are also regularly updated on the college portal.

Separate hostel facilities are available for boys and girls within the campus premises. Hostel rooms are equipped with beds, study tables, fans, Wi-Fi connectivity, and storage cupboards. The hostel also provides laundry services, recreational areas, common television rooms, study halls, purified drinking water, and 24-hour security surveillance.

The college cafeteria serves breakfast, lunch, evening snacks, beverages, and dinner at affordable prices for students and staff. Healthy meal options, vegetarian food, and hygienic cooking standards are maintained. The cafeteria remains open from 8 AM to 8 PM on all working days.

The transportation department operates buses connecting the campus with nearby cities, towns, and residential areas. Students can apply for bus passes every semester through the transport office. GPS tracking and dedicated faculty coordinators are available for student safety during transportation.

The computer science department provides access to advanced laboratories equipped with high-performance computers, internet connectivity, programming software, cloud computing platforms, and artificial intelligence development tools. Students are encouraged to work on practical projects, hackathons, and open-source contributions.

The innovation and entrepreneurship cell supports students interested in startups, business ideas, and research innovation. Students receive mentorship from faculty members and industry experts. Funding guidance, startup workshops, and entrepreneurship competitions are organized throughout the academic year.

Medical facilities are available through the student wellness center located near the main administrative block. Basic first aid, emergency healthcare assistance, counseling support, and mental wellness sessions are provided to students whenever required. Ambulance services are available for medical emergencies.

Students are encouraged to participate in technical clubs, cultural activities, sports competitions, robotics events, coding contests, and innovation challenges organized by the college. Participation in extracurricular activities helps students improve teamwork, leadership, communication, and problem-solving skills.

The college provides high-speed Wi-Fi connectivity across classrooms, laboratories, hostels, libraries, seminar halls, and common areas. Students must use their official college credentials to securely access the campus network and online learning platforms.

Laboratory facilities are available for engineering, science, management, and computer application programs. Students are required to follow laboratory safety procedures, wear proper protective equipment where necessary, and maintain practical records for internal assessment purposes.

Faculty members conduct regular mentoring sessions to guide students regarding academics, internships, placements, higher education opportunities, and personal development. Each student is assigned a faculty mentor who monitors attendance, performance, and overall progress.

The college auditorium hosts seminars, conferences, guest lectures, placement drives, technical symposiums, cultural programs, and annual day celebrations. The auditorium is equipped with modern audio systems, projectors, lighting arrangements, and seating facilities.

Online learning resources are accessible through the college learning management system portal. Students can download lecture notes, assignments, laboratory manuals, recorded video lectures, and examination materials using their student login credentials.

The college organizes industrial visits and internship programs to provide students with practical exposure to real-world work environments. Students from various departments visit industries, research centers, software companies, and manufacturing plants as part of experiential learning.

The anti-ragging committee strictly prohibits ragging activities within the campus and hostel premises. Students found violating anti-ragging rules may face disciplinary action, suspension, or legal proceedings according to institutional policies and government regulations.

Environmental sustainability initiatives are promoted through tree plantation drives, waste management programs, energy conservation awareness campaigns, and eco-friendly campus practices. Students actively participate in environmental clubs and social responsibility activities.

The college sports complex includes facilities for cricket, football, basketball, volleyball, badminton, table tennis, athletics, and indoor games. Annual sports events and intercollegiate tournaments are organized regularly to encourage physical fitness and teamwork among students.

Students can access digital payment systems for fee payments, hostel fees, transportation fees, and examination fees through the online student portal. Payment receipts and transaction history are automatically generated and stored digitally for future reference.

Research activities are encouraged among final-year and postgraduate students. Faculty members guide students in publishing research papers, participating in conferences, and developing innovative project prototypes in emerging technology domains.

The central computing center remains open during working hours to support students with software installations, account access issues, programming assistance, printing facilities, and technical troubleshooting related to academic activities.

Career guidance workshops are organized regularly to help students prepare for competitive examinations, government jobs, higher studies, entrepreneurship opportunities, and international education programs. Experts from different industries conduct motivational and career counseling sessions.

The college conducts orientation programs for first-year students to familiarize them with campus facilities, academic regulations, student services, clubs, laboratories, examination systems, and extracurricular opportunities available throughout the course duration.

Students are required to maintain discipline, follow academic integrity policies, and respect campus rules and regulations. Malpractice during examinations, misconduct, vandalism, or disruptive activities may result in disciplinary action by the college administration.

The training and placement department maintains collaborations with technology companies and industry partners to provide certification courses, internship opportunities, live projects, and skill development programs aligned with current market demands.

The college encourages participation in national-level hackathons, coding competitions, paper presentation contests, startup expos, and technical festivals. Students receive faculty guidance and institutional support for participating in such events.

Cloud computing, machine learning, cybersecurity, data analytics, and software development workshops are organized periodically to help students stay updated with emerging technologies and improve industry-relevant skills.
